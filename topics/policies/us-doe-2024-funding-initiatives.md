# US DOE 2024 Funding Initiatives for Industrial Decarbonization

## Overview

In 2024, the US Department of Energy (DOE) has significantly advanced industrial decarbonization through substantial federal investments, targeting hard-to-abate sectors such as steel, cement, chemicals, and aluminum. These initiatives aim to demonstrate scalable technologies that reduce greenhouse gas emissions while fostering domestic clean manufacturing. A key program, the Industrial Demonstrations Program (IDP), allocated up to $6 billion in March 2024 to support projects that could cut emissions by over 16 million metric tons of CO2 annually once operational. This funding builds on the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law and Inflation Reduction Act, emphasizing carbon capture, utilization, and storage (CCUS), electrification, and low-carbon fuels.

## Key Funding Allocations

The DOE selected 33 projects across 20 states, with a total federal investment of $6.3 billion matched by $4.9 billion in private funds. Notable examples include:

- **Steel Sector**: Cleveland-Cliffs' $1.2 billion project in Ohio and Indiana will produce low-carbon steel using hydrogen and electric arc furnaces, aiming to reduce emissions by 1.5 million tons per year. This initiative supports the transition from traditional blast furnaces to greener alternatives.

- **Cement Sector**: CalPortland's $1.5 billion facility in California will deploy CCUS to capture 1 million tons of CO2 annually, representing a breakthrough in low-carbon cement production. Similarly, Holcim's project in Colorado focuses on carbon capture for cement, enhancing sector-wide scalability.

- **Chemicals and Other Industries**: Projects like Air Products' $1.2 billion blue hydrogen facility in Louisiana and a $500 million advanced materials plant in Texas target petrochemical emissions through clean hydrogen and efficient processes.

These demonstrations are expected to create over 20,000 jobs and drive innovation in industrial heat pumps, thermal storage, and sustainable feedstocks.

## Policy Context and Impact

The IDP complements other 2024 policies, including the 45Q tax credits for CCUS and the Industrial Efficiency and Decarbonization Office's efforts. By addressing process emissions that electrification alone cannot mitigate, these initiatives set a "gold standard" for global clean manufacturing. Early results from similar pilots indicate potential emission reductions of 80-90% in targeted facilities, though full-scale deployment faces challenges like infrastructure needs and supply chain integration.

## Challenges and Next Steps

While promising, barriers include high upfront costs and the need for robust carbon markets. The DOE plans to monitor these projects through 2030, with opportunities for international collaboration to accelerate adoption in global supply chains.
