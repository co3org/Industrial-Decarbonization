# Refining Sector Decarbonization Pathways and Technologies

The petroleum refining industry accounts for approximately 4% of global industrial CO2 emissions, primarily from process heat, hydrogen production, and flaring. Key decarbonization pathways include electrification of process units, carbon capture and storage (CCS) on fluid catalytic cracking and hydrogen plants, and integration of green hydrogen for hydrotreating.

Recent analyses highlight that CCS and clean electricity could abate over 50% of emissions by 2030 at moderate costs, with full pathways detailed in the U.S. DOE Industrial Decarbonization Liftoff report covering refining alongside chemicals and steel. [https://www.energy.gov/sites/default/files/2024-12/itiac-march-2024-cresko-industrial-decarb-roadmap.pdf]

Emerging opportunities also involve advanced catalysts for lower-temperature operations and waste heat recovery systems, aligning with broader industrial cluster strategies already documented in this repository.
