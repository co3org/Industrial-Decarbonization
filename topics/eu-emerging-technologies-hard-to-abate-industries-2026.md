# EU JRC Factsheets on Emerging Technologies for Hard-to-Abate Industries (2026)

## Overview
In March 2026, the European Commission's Joint Research Centre (JRC) published seven sectoral factsheets mapping decarbonization pathways for key hard-to-abate industries in the EU. These factsheets identify priority emerging technologies requiring funding and innovation to achieve net-zero emissions by 2050, while enhancing industrial competitiveness. They cover technology readiness levels from 2025 to 2050 and emphasize the need for low-carbon infrastructure like hydrogen networks, CO2 storage, and grid upgrades.[JRC Report](https://joint-research-centre.ec.europa.eu/jrc-news-and-updates/industrial-decarbonisation-eu-what-emerging-technologies-need-funding-2026-03-11_en)

The factsheets highlight short-term measures such as energy efficiency, electrification, fuel switching, recycling, and circular economy practices, alongside long-term breakthroughs to avoid carbon lock-in given the sectors' long asset lifespans.

## Covered Sectors and Key Emerging Technologies

### Steel
Pathways focus on hydrogen-based direct reduction and electric arc furnaces, alongside CCUS for residual emissions. Emerging tech includes advanced electrolysis for green hydrogen production to scale low-carbon steelmaking.[JRC Report](https://joint-research-centre.ec.europa.eu/jrc-news-and-updates/industrial-decarbonisation-eu-what-emerging-technologies-need-funding-2026-03-11_en)

### Cement
Alternative binders (e.g., calcium sulfoaluminate) and novel clinkering processes aim to reduce process emissions by 50-70%. Large-scale CCS integration is critical for full decarbonization, with pilots showing feasibility in EU plants.[JRC Report](https://joint-research-centre.ec.europa.eu/jrc-news-and-updates/industrial-decarbonisation-eu-what-emerging-technologies-need-funding-2026-03-11_en)

### Ammonia
Electrolytic and plasma-based synthesis using renewable electricity and green hydrogen enable zero-emission production. These technologies target the fertilizer and chemical sectors, with TRL advancing to commercial scale by 2030.[JRC Report](https://joint-research-centre.ec.europa.eu/jrc-news-and-updates/industrial-decarbonisation-eu-what-emerging-technologies-need-funding-2026-03-11_en)

### Pulp & Paper
Biomass gasification and black liquor recovery with CCUS reduce fossil fuel use. Emerging bio-based alternatives and digital optimization for energy efficiency support Scope 1 and 2 emissions cuts.[JRC Report](https://joint-research-centre.ec.europa.eu/jrc-news-and-updates/industrial-decarbonisation-eu-what-emerging-technologies-need-funding-2026-03-11_en)

### Aluminium
Inert anode technology eliminates PFC emissions from electrolysis, enabling inert atmosphere smelting with renewable power. Recycling advancements could meet 50% of demand with low-carbon scrap by 2040.[JRC Report](https://joint-research-centre.ec.europa.eu/jrc-news-and-updates/industrial-decarbonisation-eu-what-emerging-technologies-need-funding-2026-03-11_en)

### Ceramics
Electric kilns and microwave heating replace gas firing, with fluxless sintering as an emerging low-energy process. Sector-specific CCUS for high-temperature applications is under pilot.[JRC Report](https://joint-research-centre.ec.europa.eu/jrc-news-and-updates/industrial-decarbonisation-eu-what-emerging-technologies-need-funding-2026-03-11_en)

### Glass
Oxygen-fuel combustion and electric melting reduce energy intensity by 20-30%. Advanced foam glass production and waste heat recovery integrate with hydrogen blending for deeper cuts.[JRC Report](https://joint-research-centre.ec.europa.eu/jrc-news-and-updates/industrial-decarbonisation-eu-what-emerging-technologies-need-funding-2026-03-11_en)

## Challenges and Recommendations
Common barriers include infrastructure gaps (e.g., H2 pipelines, CO2 transport) and high upfront costs. The JRC recommends targeted EU funding via Innovation Fund and Horizon Europe to bridge TRL gaps, alongside policy incentives for early adoption.[JRC Report](https://joint-research-centre.ec.europa.eu/jrc-news-and-updates/industrial-decarbonisation-eu-what-emerging-technologies-need-funding-2026-03-11_en)

This resource complements EU policies like the Industrial Accelerator Act by providing technology-specific guidance for net-zero transitions.
