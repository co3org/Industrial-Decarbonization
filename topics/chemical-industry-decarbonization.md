# Chemical Industry Decarbonization Pathways and Technologies

The chemical sector accounts for approximately 20% of global industrial CO2 emissions, primarily from process heat, feedstock use, and steam cracking. Key decarbonization pathways include electrification of processes, green hydrogen as feedstock, carbon capture and storage (CCUS), and bio-based alternatives.

## Major Pathways
- **Electrification and Efficiency**: Replacing fossil fuel-based heat with electric boilers and heat pumps, supported by high-temperature thermal storage.
- **Green Hydrogen Feedstock**: Substituting gray hydrogen in ammonia and methanol production with renewable-derived hydrogen.
- **CCUS Integration**: Capturing emissions from ethylene and ammonia plants, building on advances in steel and cement applications.
- **Circular and Bio-Based Chemistry**: Using recycled plastics and biomass to reduce virgin fossil inputs.

Recent analyses highlight that net-positive measures like efficiency and electrification could deliver 10-15% of required abatement by 2030 at low cost. [Pathways to Commercial Liftoff: Industrial Decarbonization](https://climateprogramportal.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/02/LIFTOFF_DOE_Industrial-Decarbonization_v8.pdf)

## Policy and Funding Support
US DOE programs target chemical decarbonization through efficiency and CCUS demonstrations, aligning with broader industrial funding initiatives.

## Challenges and Opportunities
High capital costs for CCUS and hydrogen infrastructure remain barriers, but industrial clusters enable shared infrastructure. Emerging EU policies accelerate permitting for strategic sectors.
