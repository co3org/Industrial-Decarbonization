# Recent Advances in Green Hydrogen for Steel Decarbonization (2026)

Green hydrogen is emerging as a pivotal technology for decarbonizing the steel industry, one of the hardest-to-abate sectors responsible for approximately 7-9% of global CO2 emissions. By replacing coal-based reduction processes with hydrogen-based direct reduced iron (DRI), steel production can achieve near-zero emissions, emitting water vapor instead of CO2. This topic explores key developments from early 2026, focusing on commercial-scale transitions and supportive policies.

## Transition to Commercial-Scale Production

Swedish steelmaker SSAB is advancing its HYBRIT initiative toward commercial hydrogen-reduced steel production starting in 2026. The project, which began as a pilot in 2021, now aims to produce fossil-free steel at scale using green hydrogen generated from renewable electricity. This milestone represents a shift from demonstration to market-ready operations, with initial output targeted at 2.5 million tons annually by the late 2020s.[SSAB's HYBRIT Progress](https://www.theclimategroup.org/our-work/news/2026-year-rules-are-changing-steel-and-concrete)

Similarly, Stegra (formerly H2 Green Steel) secured a multi-year supply agreement with Thyssenkrupp in early 2026 to deliver green steel produced via hydrogen-DRI processes. The Boden, Sweden facility is on track for operational startup in 2026, leveraging electrolytic hydrogen to reduce emissions by over 95% compared to traditional blast furnaces. This deal underscores growing buyer confidence and the integration of hydrogen infrastructure in European steel hubs.[Stegra-Thyssenkrupp Agreement](https://www.canarymedia.com/articles/green-steel/stegra-lands-key-customer-steel-plant)

## Policy and Market Support

The European Union's Net-Zero Industry Act and Innovation Fund are accelerating hydrogen adoption in steel, with €2.7 billion awarded in 2026 for clean industry projects including hydrogen-DRI facilities. In parallel, India's February 2026 budget allocated ₹20,000 crore (approximately $2.4 billion) over five years for CCUS and hydrogen integration in steel and cement sectors, aiming for 30% emission reductions by 2030.[India's CCUS and Hydrogen Funding](https://discoveryalert.com.au/carbon-capture-utilisation-storage-2026-technology-integration)

Globally, the CCUS hydrogen supply market for steel is projected to reach $2.4 billion in 2026, growing at 11% CAGR through 2036, driven by cluster-based infrastructure and policy incentives.[Market Projections](https://www.futuremarketinsights.com/reports/ccus-hydrogen-supply-for-steel-and-heavy-industry-market)

## Challenges and Outlook

Key hurdles include scaling green hydrogen production to meet steel's demand (estimated at 12-15 million tons annually by 2050) and ensuring cost-competitiveness below $2/kg. Despite these, 2026 marks a tipping point with multiple projects moving beyond pilots, supported by international roadmaps from the IEA and World Steel Association.[IEA Roadmap](https://worldsteel.org/climate-action/breakthrough-technologies)

This advancement complements existing CCUS approaches, offering a dual-pathway strategy for steel decarbonization.
