# High-Temperature Thermal Energy Storage for Industrial Decarbonization (2026)

## Overview

High-temperature thermal energy storage (TES) technologies, including thermal batteries, are emerging as a key enabler for decarbonizing industrial heat processes that require temperatures above 500°C, such as in cement, steel, and chemicals production. These systems store excess renewable energy as heat in materials like molten salts, ceramics, or metals, then dispatch it on demand to replace fossil fuel combustion. In 2026, TES is gaining traction amid grid constraints and the need for 24/7 clean heat, with pilots demonstrating scalability and cost reductions.[Thermal storage heats up as industry hunts for cheaper, cleaner energy](https://www.reuters.com/sustainability/climate-energy/thermal-storage-heats-up-industry-hunts-cheaper-cleaner-energy--ecmii-2026-04-21)

Fossil fuels still power about 80% of industrial heat globally, making TES essential for avoiding emissions lock-in while integrating intermittent renewables. Market projections indicate the TES sector could reach $53.4 billion by 2030, driven by industrial decarbonization imperatives.[Thermal energy storage tech landscape 2026](https://www.patsnap.com/resources/blog/articles/thermal-energy-storage-tech-landscape-2026)

## Key Technologies

### Thermal Batteries

Thermal batteries store energy in solid or phase-change materials at temperatures up to 1,800°C, using resistive heating from surplus electricity. Companies like Antora Energy and Rondo Energy are deploying modular units that can hold heat for days, with round-trip efficiencies over 90%. These are suitable for retrofitting existing furnaces, reducing capex by 30-50% compared to full electrification.[2026 Watchlist: Industrial Decarbonization Technologies to Pilot](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/2026-watchlist-industrial-decarbonization-technologies-pilot-greyb-7tuyc)

### Molten Salt and Ceramic Storage

Molten salts (e.g., sodium-potassium nitrate) store heat at 500-1,000°C for applications like cement kilns. Advanced ceramics enable higher temperatures without corrosion, supporting steel reheating. Pilot projects in Europe and the US show integration with solar thermal for hybrid systems.[Thermal Batteries—A New Challenger in Sustainable Energy Storage for Heat-Intensive Industries](https://www.arcweb.com/blog/thermal-batteries-new-challenger-sustainable-energy-storage-heat-intensive-industries)

### Industrial Solar Thermal with Storage

Concentrated solar power (CSP) paired with TES provides dispatchable heat up to 1,000°C. Innovations in heliostat designs and storage media are lowering costs, with applications in glass and ceramics manufacturing.[2026 Watchlist: Industrial Decarbonization Technologies to Pilot](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/2026-watchlist-industrial-decarbonization-technologies-pilot-greyb-7tuyc)

## Applications in Hard-to-Abate Sectors

- **Cement and Lime**: TES replaces natural gas in preheaters and kilns, capturing waste heat for reuse and cutting emissions by 50-70% in pilots.
- **Steel**: Provides consistent heat for rolling mills and forging, complementing hydrogen DRI by stabilizing renewable inputs.
- **Chemicals and Petrochemicals**: Enables low-carbon steam generation for crackers and reformers.
- **Food Processing and Textiles**: Lower-temperature variants (300-500°C) support drying and curing processes.

These technologies support industrial symbiosis in clusters, where shared TES infrastructure reduces costs.[Thermal batteries for industrial heat and power](https://initiatives.weforum.org/future-power-system/case-study-details/thermal-batteries-for-industrial-heat-and-power/aJYTG00000013of4AA)

## Challenges and Outlook

Barriers include material durability at extreme temperatures and integration with legacy equipment. Policy support via subsidies (e.g., US IRA tax credits) and R&D funding is accelerating commercialization, with first large-scale deployments expected by 2028. TES could abate 10-15% of industrial heat emissions by 2030 when combined with electrification and biofuels.[Thermal storage heats up as industry hunts for cheaper, cleaner energy](https://www.reuters.com/sustainability/climate-energy/thermal-storage-heats-up-industry-hunts-cheaper-cleaner-energy--ecmii-2026-04-21)

This complements existing strategies like CCUS and green hydrogen, offering a cost-effective bridge to full net-zero.
