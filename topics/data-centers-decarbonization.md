# Data Centers and Digital Infrastructure Decarbonization Pathways

Data centers and digital infrastructure account for a rapidly growing share of industrial and energy-related emissions, driven by AI, cloud computing, and 5G expansion. Global data center electricity consumption is projected to reach 8-10% of world electricity by 2030 in some scenarios, with direct and indirect emissions requiring targeted strategies beyond traditional manufacturing sectors.

## Key Challenges
- High power density and 24/7 baseload demand
- Scope 3 emissions from supply chains and embodied carbon in servers
- Water usage for cooling in water-stressed regions
- Rapid growth outpacing grid decarbonization in some regions

## Decarbonization Pathways
### Energy Efficiency and Optimization
- Advanced liquid cooling, immersion cooling, and waste heat recovery
- AI-optimized workload scheduling and predictive maintenance
- Free cooling and evaporative systems with efficiency metrics (PUE <1.2)

### Renewable Energy Procurement and Onsite Generation
- 24/7 carbon-free energy matching via PPAs, PPAs with storage, and behind-the-meter renewables
- Examples: Google's and Microsoft's hourly matching commitments

### Electrification and Grid Integration
- Grid-interactive data centers providing demand flexibility
- Integration with industrial clusters for shared infrastructure

### Emerging Technologies
- Advanced nuclear (SMRs) for dedicated clean power
- Geothermal and other firm clean sources
- Carbon capture for backup diesel generators

## Case Studies and Initiatives
- Microsoft zero-water data centers and AI efficiency gains
- Equinix and Digital Realty renewable targets
- Emerging policy support in US DOE and EU programs

## Policy and Finance Enablers
- State-level incentives and federal tax credits for clean energy
- Corporate renewable procurement standards

*Sources: IEA reports, DOE analyses, company sustainability disclosures (2025-2026 updates).*
