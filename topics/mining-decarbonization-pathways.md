# Mining Sector Decarbonization Pathways and Technologies

Mining is a critical enabler of the energy transition but also a significant source of emissions, primarily from diesel-powered equipment, electricity use in processing, and fugitive methane. Decarbonizing mining supports supply of critical minerals while aligning with net-zero goals.

## Key Challenges

- High reliance on diesel for haul trucks and mobile equipment.
- Energy-intensive comminution and processing.
- Scope 3 emissions from supply chains and downstream use.
- Remote locations limiting grid access.

## Decarbonization Pathways

- **Electrification of mobile equipment**: Battery-electric and trolley-assist haul trucks (e.g., BHP and Fortescue initiatives).
- **Renewable energy integration**: Onsite solar/wind with storage and microgrids for operations.
- **Energy efficiency and process optimization**: Advanced grinding technologies and digital twins.
- **Hydrogen and alternative fuels**: Green hydrogen for high-duty cycles where electrification is challenging.
- **Carbon capture and nature-based solutions**: For processing emissions and site rehabilitation.

Recent analyses highlight opportunities in electric ultra-class trucks and consolidated standards for sustainable mining. [Mining Decarbonization Report](https://climateinstitute.ca/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/Mining-decarbonization.pdf) [IIS D Report on Mining Decarbonization](https://www.iisd.org/publications/report/igf-decarbonization-mining-sector)

## Policy and Funding Support

- National policies in Australia, Canada, and Chile targeting mining emissions.
- Incentives for critical minerals extraction with low-carbon requirements.
