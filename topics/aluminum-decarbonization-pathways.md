# Aluminum Sector Decarbonization Pathways and Technologies

Aluminum production is one of the hard-to-abate industrial sectors, accounting for significant process emissions primarily from the Hall-Héroult electrolysis process and energy-intensive smelting. Global aluminum demand is projected to grow, making decarbonization critical for net-zero goals.

## Key Challenges
- High electricity consumption in primary aluminum production (smelting).
- Process emissions from carbon anodes (CO2 and perfluorocarbons).
- Bauxite mining and alumina refining emissions.

## Decarbonization Pathways
- **Inert anodes**: Replacing carbon anodes with inert materials to eliminate process CO2 emissions (e.g., ELYSIS technology).
- **Renewable and low-carbon electricity**: Shifting smelters to hydro, solar, or wind power; many existing facilities already use hydro.
- **Recycling and secondary aluminum**: Increasing scrap recycling rates, which uses ~5% of the energy of primary production.
- **Carbon capture**: Potential application at refineries and smelters, though less mature than in cement/steel.
- **Alternative processes**: Emerging technologies like carbothermic reduction or hydrogen-based reduction.

## Policy and Funding Support
- EU Innovation Fund and Clean Industrial Deal initiatives target low-carbon aluminum technologies.
- US DOE and IRA incentives for clean energy and industrial efficiency apply to aluminum facilities.
