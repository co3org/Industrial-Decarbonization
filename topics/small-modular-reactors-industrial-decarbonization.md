# Small Modular Reactors for Industrial Heat and Power Decarbonization

Small modular reactors (SMRs) offer a promising pathway for providing reliable, low-carbon heat and electricity to hard-to-abate industrial sectors such as chemicals, steel, and cement. Unlike intermittent renewables, SMRs can deliver baseload power and high-temperature process heat directly at industrial sites, reducing reliance on fossil fuels for both energy and feedstock needs.

Recent analyses highlight SMR deployments for replacing natural gas in chemical production and enabling hydrogen co-production. For example, Dow Chemicals is exploring high-temperature gas-cooled SMRs at US production sites to decarbonize operations. [https://www.iaea.org/bulletin/the-energy-transition-and-industrial-decarbonization](https://www.iaea.org/bulletin/the-energy-transition-and-industrial-decarbonization)

Key advantages include:
- High-temperature output (up to 700°C+) suitable for process heat
- Factory fabrication for faster deployment and lower costs
- Integration with industrial clusters for shared infrastructure

Challenges remain around regulatory approval, supply chain development, and public acceptance, but pilot projects are advancing in the US, Canada, and Europe as of 2026.
