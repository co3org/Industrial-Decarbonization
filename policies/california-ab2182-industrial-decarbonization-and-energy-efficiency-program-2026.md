# California AB 2182: Industrial Decarbonization and Energy Efficiency Program (2026)

## Overview

Assembly Bill 2182 (AB 2182) mandates that large electrical corporations in California establish an Industrial Decarbonization and Energy Efficiency Program to provide grants for emission-reducing projects in industrial and manufacturing facilities. This initiative leverages utility-collected funds to accelerate decarbonization efforts in hard-to-abate sectors, aligning with California's broader climate goals under the Cap-and-Trade Program and net-zero targets.

## Key Provisions

- **Program Establishment**: Each large electrical corporation must submit a Tier 2 advice letter to the California Public Utilities Commission (CPUC) by August 1, 2027, detailing the program's structure and administration.
- **Funding Mechanism**: Funding derives from energy efficiency charges imposed on eligible industrial and manufacturing facilities that meet specific consumption and operational criteria.
- **Grant Awards**: Grants support eligible projects focused on energy efficiency and decarbonization, such as electrification, process optimizations, and renewable integration, subject to CPUC approval.
- **Administration and Oversight**: Electrical corporations manage program implementation, while the Governor's Office of Business and Economic Development conducts independent reviews and approves grants to ensure alignment with state priorities.
- **Eligibility**: Targets facilities in sectors like manufacturing, chemicals, and food processing that demonstrate potential for significant greenhouse gas reductions.

## Legislative Status

Introduced in the 2025-2026 session, AB 2182 was amended on April 13, 2026, passed the Assembly Committees on Natural Resources (April 20, 2026) and Utilities and Energy (April 8, 2026), and re-referred to the Committee on Appropriations as of May 2026. If enacted, the program will operationalize in 2027, providing a new funding avenue distinct from the Manufacturing Decarbonization Incentive Fund (MDIF).

## Expected Impact

The program is projected to mobilize millions in grants annually, enabling industrial facilities to adopt low-carbon technologies and reduce emissions by targeting energy-intensive processes. By integrating decarbonization into utility rate structures, it addresses barriers to adoption in California's industrial base, potentially cutting sector-wide emissions by 5-10% over the next decade through efficiency gains and clean energy shifts.
