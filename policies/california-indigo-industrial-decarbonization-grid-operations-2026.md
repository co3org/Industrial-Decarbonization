# California INDIGO Program: Industrial Decarbonization and Improvement of Grid Operations (2026)

The Industrial Decarbonization and Improvement of Grid Operations (INDIGO) Program, administered by the California Energy Commission (CEC), provides financial incentives for projects at industrial facilities that deliver significant benefits to the electrical grid, reduce greenhouse gas emissions, achieve the state’s clean energy goals, and exceed compliance requirements. [https://www.energy.ca.gov/programs-and-topics/programs/industrial-decarbonization-and-improvement-grid-operations-indigo](https://www.energy.ca.gov/programs-and-topics/programs/industrial-decarbonization-and-improvement-grid-operations-indigo)

Established under Assembly Bill 209 (Chapter 251, Statutes of 2021), INDIGO targets manufacturing, production, and processing facilities (excluding food and beverage in some solicitations). Recent rounds include GFO-23-313 for deployment of decarbonization technologies, with awards supporting electrification, efficiency, and grid-interactive projects. [https://www.grants.ca.gov/grants/cost-share-for-federal-funding-opportunities-industrial-decarbonization-and-improvement-of-grid-operations-indigo-program-and-food-production-investment-program-fpip/](https://www.grants.ca.gov/grants/cost-share-for-federal-funding-opportunities-industrial-decarbonization-and-improvement-of-grid-operations-indigo-program-and-food-production-investment-program-fpip/)

In 2025-2026, AB 1280 expanded eligibility to include thermal energy storage and industrial heat pumps, enhancing support for grid-beneficial clean heat solutions. Multiple funding opportunities have reopened or advanced, including cost-share for federal programs and direct grants up to $10M in recent rounds. [https://www.manufacturingdive.com/news/california-bill-ab1280-expands-incentives-heat-pumps-thermal-energy-storage-indigo/759975/](https://www.manufacturingdive.com/news/california-bill-ab1280-expands-incentives-heat-pumps-thermal-energy-storage-indigo/759975/)

This complements existing California policies like AB 2182 and the Manufacturing Decarbonization Incentive Fund by emphasizing grid integration alongside emissions reductions.
