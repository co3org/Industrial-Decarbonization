# EU Innovation Fund 2026: €2.7 Billion for 54 Clean Industry Projects

## Overview

On March 24, 2026, the European Commission announced the signing of grant agreements for 54 innovative clean industry projects under the Innovation Fund, allocating €2.7 billion to accelerate the deployment of net-zero technologies across Europe.[Commission unlocks €2.7 billion for 54 clean industry projects under the Innovation Fund](https://cinea.ec.europa.eu/news-events/news/commission-unlocks-eu27-billion-54-clean-industry-projects-under-innovation-fund-2026-03-24_en) This funding, drawn from revenues of the EU Emissions Trading System (EU ETS), supports the EU's transition to a climate-neutral economy by 2050, targeting hard-to-abate industrial sectors and emerging cleantech manufacturing.

The Innovation Fund, with a total budget of €40 billion from 2020 to 2030, prioritizes large-scale demonstrations of low-carbon technologies that are not yet commercially viable. This round stems from the 2024 Call for Net-Zero Technologies (IF24 Call), launched on December 4, 2025, with an initial €2.9 billion budget. Grants range from €1.8 million to €216 million per project, fostering innovation in energy-intensive industries.[EU allocates EUR2.7bn to accelerate clean industry transition](https://www.enerdata.net/publications/daily-energy-news/eu-allocates-eur27bn-accelerate-clean-industry-transition.html)

## Key Details

The 54 projects are distributed across 17 EU countries and span 17 industrial sectors, including:
- Refineries, cement, lime, and glass manufacturing
- Chemical and critical raw material processing (e.g., lithium refining for EV batteries)
- Renewable energy components (solar, wind, electrolysers)
- Net-zero mobility (maritime, aviation, road transport)
- Cleantech manufacturing and pilot-scale innovations

Additionally, six projects from the reserve list have been invited to prepare grant agreements, potentially unlocking up to €491 million more. These initiatives aim to bridge the gap between research and market-ready solutions, with a focus on scaling technologies like carbon capture, green hydrogen production, and advanced materials recycling.

## Expected Impacts

Over the first ten years of operation, the 54 projects are projected to avoid approximately 210 million tonnes of CO₂ emissions, equivalent to the annual emissions of several mid-sized EU countries. The reserve projects could add another 20 million tonnes of avoidance. Beyond emissions reductions, these investments are expected to create thousands of high-skilled jobs, enhance energy security, and stimulate private sector co-financing, with total project costs exceeding €10 billion when including leveraged investments.[EU Commission Approves €2.7B for 54 Clean Industry Projects](https://news.fundsforngos.org/2026/03/25/eu-commission-approves-e2-7b-for-54-clean-industry-projects)

## Connection to Broader EU Decarbonization Efforts

This funding complements the recently proposed Industrial Accelerator Act (March 4, 2026), which aims to boost demand for low-carbon European-made products and streamline permitting for clean tech deployments. Together, these measures address key barriers in industrial decarbonization, such as high upfront costs and technology risks, aligning with the EU's goal of €660 billion annual investments in clean energy through 2030. By supporting diverse sectors, the Innovation Fund helps ensure equitable progress toward net-zero emissions in heavy industry.
