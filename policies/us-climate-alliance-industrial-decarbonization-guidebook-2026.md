# US Climate Alliance Enabling Industrial Decarbonization Policy Guidebook (2026 Update)

The U.S. Climate Alliance released an updated *Enabling Industrial Decarbonization: A Policy Guidebook for U.S. States* in February 2026. It details strategies, pathways, and over 170 state policies (including 60 new additions since 2022) to reduce GHG emissions from the industrial sector, with a new Policy Dashboard for visualization.

## Key Sections and Focus Areas
- Sector-specific barriers and opportunities (e.g., chemicals, steel, cement, refining).
- Policy instruments: financial incentives, regulatory standards (including Buy Clean and clean product standards), technical assistance, workforce development, and market creation via procurement.
- Emphasis on multi-stakeholder approaches, timing/sequencing of policies, and integration with federal programs like IRA and IIJA.
- Examples from states such as California, Colorado, New York, and others on embodied carbon disclosure, low-carbon procurement, and efficiency programs.

## Relevance to Industrial Decarbonization
This guidebook serves as a comprehensive state-level policy resource, complementing federal and international efforts. It highlights how states can drive demand for low-carbon materials, support facility-level technology deployment, and align with broader climate goals.
