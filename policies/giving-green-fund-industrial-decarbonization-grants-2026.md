# Giving Green Fund Grants for Industrial Decarbonization (February 2026)

In February 2026, the Giving Green Fund announced $1.94 million in grants to support clean energy integration and heavy industry decarbonization in the United States. This funding aligns with Giving Green's strategies for unleashing clean energy and decarbonizing heavy industries such as cement, steel, chemicals, and aluminum, including exploration of geologic hydrogen as a low-carbon fuel source.[Giving Green Fund Announcement](https://www.givinggreen.earth/post/giving-green-fund-announces-1-94m-in-february-2026-grants-for-clean-energy-and-industrial-decarbonization)

## Grant Breakdown

### Unleashing Clean Energy in the U.S. ($900,000 Total)
These grants focus on policy and infrastructure barriers to clean energy deployment, including federal permitting reform, grid modernization, and financing for clean firm power.
- **Bipartisan Policy Center**: $300,000 for federal permitting reform to accelerate clean energy projects.
- **Employ America**: $200,000 for interconnection and grid modernization efforts.
- **Institute for Progress** and **Good Energy Collective**: $200,000 each for clean firm financing initiatives.

### Decarbonizing Heavy Industry ($1.041 Million Total)
These grants target emissions reductions in hard-to-abate sectors through advocacy, research, and early technology development.
- **Industrious Labs**: $400,000 to advance decarbonization policies and technologies in cement, steel, chemicals, and aluminum industries.[Industrious Labs Spotlight](https://www.givinggreen.earth/research/industrious-labs-grantee-spotlight-2026)
- **The Chimaera Fund**: $500,000 for heavy industry decarbonization, including geologic hydrogen exploration to address process emissions.[Chimaera Fund Spotlight](https://www.givinggreen.earth/research/chimaera-fund-grantee-spotlight-2026)
- **Future Cleantech Architects**: $141,000 to support innovation in cleantech architectures for industrial applications.[FCA Spotlight](https://www.givinggreen.earth/research/future-cleantech-architects-top-climate-nonprofit-spotlight)

## Goals and Impact
The grants aim to bridge funding gaps in philanthropic support for industrial decarbonization, which remains underfunded at approximately 2.6% of total foundation climate funding. By supporting nonprofits that advocate for increased RD&D funding, low-carbon commitments, and policy reforms, these initiatives are expected to accelerate the commercialization and deployment of technologies needed for net-zero emissions in heavy industries.[Giving Green Heavy Industry Strategy](https://www.givinggreen.earth/research/decarbonizing-heavy-industry)

This funding complements government programs by focusing on advocacy and early-stage innovation, particularly in underrepresented areas like chemical and cement decarbonization outside major economies.
