# EU Clean Industrial Deal 2026

The Clean Industrial Deal, proposed by the European Commission in 2026, aims to accelerate industrial decarbonization while enhancing competitiveness. It is structured around six pillars focusing on clean tech deployment, state aid frameworks, and financing mechanisms.

Key elements include:
- A dedicated €450 million call under Horizon Europe for research and innovation in decarbonization technologies.
- A new Clean Industrial Deal state aid framework to expedite approvals for renewable energy, industrial decarbonization, and clean tech manufacturing.
- Amendments to the InvestEU Regulation to mobilize up to €50 billion for clean tech, clean mobility, and waste reduction.
- Strengthening of the Innovation Fund and proposal for an industrial decarbonisation bank targeting €100 billion in funding.

This builds on existing EU initiatives like the Innovation Fund and Industrial Decarbonisation Bank. [Source](https://commission.europa.eu/topics/competitiveness/clean-industrial-deal_en)
