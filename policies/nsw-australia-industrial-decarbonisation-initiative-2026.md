# NSW Industrial Decarbonisation Initiative (2026)

The NSW Government launched the Industrial Decarbonisation Initiative to co-fund emissions reduction projects across manufacturing, mining, and other industrial sectors. It supports the state's targets of a 70% emissions reduction by 2035 and net zero by 2050, helping industries remain competitive during the transition.

Key component: The Low Emissions Industry Program provides $40 million to co-fund projects at facilities emitting up to 100,000 tCO2-e per year, including renewable gas production for industrial off-takers.

[Source](https://www.energy.nsw.gov.au/business-and-industry/programs-grants-and-schemes/industrial-decarbonisation-initiative)

Webinars and application details were active in March–May 2026, with ongoing rounds expected.

This complements existing Australian pathways reports emphasizing up to 92% industrial emissions cuts by 2050 with significant job creation potential.

[Source](https://www.energy-transitions.org/publications/pathways-to-industrial-decarbonisation/)
