# France €1.6 Billion Investment in Industrial Site Decarbonization (2026)

France has announced a major public funding initiative to accelerate decarbonization in its industrial sector by investing €1.6 billion over 15 years in projects at seven key industrial sites. This program aims to support the transition to low-carbon operations in hard-to-abate industries, contributing to national and EU climate goals.

## Key Details
- **Funding Amount**: €1.6 billion in public funds.
- **Duration**: 15 years, starting from 2026.
- **Objective**: To finance decarbonization projects that reduce greenhouse gas emissions from energy-intensive industrial processes.
- **Eligibility**: Focused on industrial sites with high emission profiles, emphasizing technologies and processes that enable net-zero transitions.

## Targeted Industrial Sites
The initiative targets the following seven sites, involving major players in cement, chemicals, aluminum, and other sectors:
- Holcim (cement production)
- Heidelberg Materials France (cement and building materials)
- Ineos Naphtachimie (chemicals)
- Vicat (cement)
- Eurolysine (biochemicals)
- Aluminium Dunkerque (aluminum smelting)
- Specialty Operations France (Syensqo) (specialty chemicals)

These sites represent critical areas for industrial decarbonization, where innovations in carbon capture, electrification, and alternative fuels can have significant impact.

## Context and Impact
Announced by the Élysée Palace, this investment underscores France's commitment to industrial competitiveness alongside environmental sustainability. It complements broader EU efforts, such as the Innovation Fund, by providing targeted national support for site-specific projects. The program is expected to create jobs, foster technological innovation, and position French industries as leaders in sustainable manufacturing.
