# US State-Level Initiatives and Policies for Industrial Decarbonization (2025-2026)

Industrial decarbonization at the state level is gaining momentum, with states leveraging federal funding like the Climate Pollution Reduction Grants (CPRG) to develop targeted plans for reducing emissions in manufacturing, chemicals, and other heavy sectors. While federal policies provide a framework, states are innovating with incentives for electrification, low-carbon fuels, and efficiency upgrades to address local industry needs and boost competitiveness in low-emission markets.

## Key Developments in 2025-2026

### Comprehensive Climate Action Plans (CCAPs)
States are using CPRG funds to create CCAPs that include industrial measures. As of early 2026, only about 25 states have committed to specific industrial actions in their Priority Climate Action Plans (PCAPs), focusing on subsectors like steel and cement. These plans emphasize modernizing manufacturing through electrification and carbon capture to capture growing demand for low-carbon products.[<a href="https://rmi.org/insight/states-can-boost-competitiveness-by-modernizing-industry">RMI, 2026</a>]

### Illinois: Thermal Energy Networks and Jobs Act (HB 3609)
Enacted in 2025, this act allows investor-owned utilities to pilot up to three thermal energy network projects, including one industrial pilot per territory. It supports workforce training for clean energy infrastructure and includes protections for ratepayers, aiming to decarbonize industrial heating processes.[<a href="https://buildingdecarb.org/momentum-q1-2025">Building Decarbonization Coalition, 2025</a>]

### Washington State: Leading on Industrial Pathways
Washington's 2025 initiatives draw from successful models like electrified processes and green hydrogen hubs, informed by state-led roadmaps. The state is prioritizing industrial clusters for demand flexibility and renewable integration to reduce emissions in refining and materials production.[<a href="https://rmi.org/states-decarbonization-washington-industry">RMI, 2025</a>]

### Emerging Trends Across States
In 2025, states advanced clean energy deployment with policies for industrial efficiency and CCUS incentives. California continues funding deployment of decarbonization technologies through solicitations like the 2026 Industrial Decarbonization program, targeting cutting-edge solutions in high-emission sectors.[<a href="https://www.catf.us/2026/01/2025-wins-emerging-trends-state-climate-policy">CATF, 2026</a>] Other states are exploring thermal networks and grants to scale low-carbon infrastructure.[<a href="https://usclimatealliance.org/guide/industrial-decarbonization-guide-dec-2022">US Climate Alliance, 2022 (updated 2026)</a>]

## Challenges and Opportunities
State policies face barriers like grid constraints but offer opportunities for blended finance and public-private partnerships. By aligning with federal incentives, states can accelerate adoption of technologies like electrified heating, potentially cutting industrial emissions by 20-30% by 2030.

*Sources: Based on recent analyses from RMI, CATF, and US Climate Alliance.*
