# Recent US Federal Funding for Industrial Decarbonization Initiatives (2024)

Industrial decarbonization requires substantial investment in innovative technologies and infrastructure. In 2024, the US Department of Energy (DOE) has allocated significant funding to advance emissions reduction in hard-to-abate sectors like cement, steel, chemicals, and manufacturing. This document summarizes key funding announcements from the past year, focusing on opportunities for research, demonstration, and deployment.

## Overview of DOE's Industrial Efficiency and Decarbonization Office (IEDO)

The DOE's IEDO leads efforts to eliminate industrial greenhouse gas emissions through cost-effective technologies. In fiscal year 2024, IEDO announced multiple funding opportunities totaling over $180 million, targeting energy-intensive industries and cross-sector innovations. These initiatives align with the Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) and Bipartisan Infrastructure Law (BIL), providing grants, loans, and tax incentives to scale low-carbon solutions [DOE Industrial Decarbonization Overview](https://www.energy.gov/industrial-technologies/industrial-decarbonization).

## Key Funding Announcements in 2024

### Industrial Cross-Sector Technologies Funding Opportunity

In 2024, DOE selected 16 projects under a $38 million funding opportunity to advance cross-sector technologies for industrial decarbonization. Projects focus on electrification, process heating, and carbon capture, utilization, and storage (CCUS). Examples include innovations in low-carbon cement production and efficient heat pumps for chemical processing. This funding supports demonstration-scale projects to bridge the gap between R&D and commercial deployment [DOE 2024 Industrial Cross-Sector Selections](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/ito/industrial-funding-selections-2024-industrial-cross-sector-technologies-funding).

### Energy and Emissions Intensive Industries FOA

The FY24 Energy and Emissions Intensive Industries Funding Opportunity Announcement (FOA) targets decarbonization in sectors like cement, concrete, forest products, and glass. With up to $75 million available, it emphasizes applied R&D for building materials and biofuels integration. Notable selections include projects for alternative binders in cement to reduce clinker use by up to 50%, potentially cutting emissions significantly [DOE IEDO FY24 FOA](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/ito/iedo-fy24-energy-and-emissions-intensive-industries-foa).

### Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) Awards

On September 3, 2024, DOE awarded $142 million to small businesses across 34 states, including $3.4 million specifically for industrial decarbonization technologies. These Phase I grants support early-stage innovations like advanced membranes for CCUS and AI-optimized energy efficiency in manufacturing. This program fosters entrepreneurship in clean tech, with potential for follow-on Phase II funding up to $1.1 million per project [DOE SBIR Awards 2024](https://www.energy.gov/industrial-technologies/listings/industrial-decarbonization-technology-news).

## Sector-Specific Impacts

- **Cement and Concrete**: Funding prioritizes low-carbon alternatives, such as alkali-activated materials, which could reduce sector emissions (8% of global CO2) by 78-88% through process changes [Cement Decarbonization Case Study](https://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.1088/1748-9326/acf6d5).
- **Chemicals and Materials**: Investments in green hydrogen and electrification aim to displace fossil fuels, with projects demonstrating cost parity in production.
- **Forest Products and Steel**: Emphasis on biomass integration and hydrogen-based reduction to achieve net-zero pathways.

## Application and Next Steps

Eligible applicants include businesses, universities, and nonprofits. Deadlines vary, but many FOAs remain open or have rolling submissions. For details, visit the DOE's funding portal and consult IRA tax credits like 45Q for CCUS [DOE Funding Opportunities](https://www.energy.gov/funding-opportunities).

These initiatives represent a surge in federal support, enabling the US to lead in industrial net-zero transitions while creating jobs and enhancing energy security.
