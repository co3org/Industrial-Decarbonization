# Germany €5 Billion Second Round of Carbon Contracts for Difference Scheme for Industrial Decarbonization (2026)

## Overview
In May 2026, the German Federal Ministry for Economic Affairs and Climate Action launched the second round of the Carbon Contracts for Difference (CCfD) scheme, allocating up to €5 billion ($5.85 billion) in state aid to support energy-intensive industries in reducing CO2 emissions. This initiative, approved by the European Commission, aims to bridge the cost gap between conventional and low-carbon production processes, enabling factories to switch to climate-friendly technologies such as electrification, hydrogen use, and carbon capture.<grok:render type="render_inline_citation"><argument name="citation_id">71</argument></grok:render>

The scheme is part of Germany's broader strategy to meet its 2030 climate targets and the EU's net-zero goals by 2050, focusing on sectors like steel, chemicals, and cement that face high decarbonization costs due to carbon pricing under the EU Emissions Trading System (ETS).<grok:render type="render_inline_citation"><argument name="citation_id">73</argument></grok:render>

## Key Details
- **Funding Amount**: €5 billion for 2026, with potential for additional rounds.
- **Mechanism**: Carbon Contracts for Difference provide long-term contracts (up to 15 years) that compensate companies for the difference between the carbon price and a reference price, reducing investment risks.<grok:render type="render_inline_citation"><argument name="citation_id">74</argument></grok:render>
- **Target Sectors**: Energy-intensive industries including basic materials (steel, cement), chemicals, paper, glass, and ceramics.
- **Application Process**: Companies can apply via the German Energy and Climate Fund (Klima- und Transformationsfonds, KTF). The first round in 2024 supported 15 companies with €2.8 billion; this second round builds on that success.<grok:render type="render_inline_citation"><argument name="citation_id">70</argument></grok:render>
- **Expected Impact**: Projected to reduce up to 20 million tons of CO2 emissions annually and create jobs in green manufacturing.

## Eligibility and Requirements
Eligible projects must demonstrate significant CO2 reductions (at least 50% compared to baseline) and align with EU state aid rules. Applicants need to provide detailed investment plans, including technology assessments and financial modeling. The scheme prioritizes projects that enhance EU competitiveness and supply chain resilience.<grok:render type="render_inline_citation"><argument name="citation_id">72</argument></grok:render>

## Background and Context
This follows the 2024 subsidies and responds to industry calls for stable support amid volatile energy prices and ETS costs. The EU Commission's approval ensures compliance with the Green Deal Industrial Plan, emphasizing just transition for workers in affected regions.<grok:render type="render_inline_citation"><argument name="citation_id">71</argument></grok:render>
