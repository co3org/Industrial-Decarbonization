# US DOE $3M Industrial Systems Energy Efficiency Development (ISEED) Collaborative (April 2026)

On April 30, 2026, the U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) announced $3 million in funding for the Industrial Systems Energy Efficiency Development (ISEED) Collaborative to support and expand the industrial decarbonization workforce. This initiative, managed by CONNECTWERX in partnership with DOE, aims to develop and disseminate instructional curricula and training programs focused on industrial energy efficiency, empowering workers to build skills for sustainable manufacturing and net-zero goals by 2050.[<a href="https://www.energy.gov/cmei/ito/articles/us-department-energy-announces-3-million-new-initiative-support-and-expand">DOE Announcement</a>]

## Objectives
- Improve energy efficiency in the U.S. industrial sector through workforce development.
- Create training programs for workers at all education levels, covering energy management, waste heat utilization, smart manufacturing, industrial electrification (e.g., heat pumps, hydrogen production), and low-carbon fuels/feedstocks (e.g., renewables, biofuels).
- Amplify existing programs, develop content for emerging technologies, align training with industry needs, and enhance accessibility for underserved communities.
- Select up to six ISEED Collaborative members to pilot scalable workforce solutions over two years, with potential for regional or national expansion.

## Funding Details
- Total available: $3 million.
- Awards will provide funding, technical support, and guidance to selected organizations.
- Focus areas include energy efficiency for existing processes, electrification of thermal processes, and adoption of low-carbon energy sources.

## Eligibility
Eligible organizations must have experience or networks in industrial energy efficiency and decarbonization, including:
- Manufacturing education and training organizations (e.g., community colleges, trade schools, excluding high schools and universities).
- Trade, industry, and labor groups.
- Organizations promoting job access for diverse populations.
- Professional societies or associations.

## Application Process and Deadlines
- Applications due: November 1, 2024, at 5:00 p.m. ET (note: verify if extended or renewed for 2026 applicants).
- Informational webinar: October 9, 2024, at 2:00 p.m. ET.
- Office hours: October 23, 2024, at 2:00 p.m. ET.
- Submit proposals via the [CONNECTWERX website](https://www.connectwerx.com/iseed-collaborative).

This initiative complements broader DOE efforts like the Industrial Decarbonization Roadmap by addressing human capital needs for deploying technologies such as CCUS, green hydrogen, and electrification in hard-to-abate sectors.
