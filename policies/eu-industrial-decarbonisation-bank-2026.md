# EU Industrial Decarbonisation Bank (IDB) 2026

The EU Industrial Decarbonisation Bank (IDB) is a proposed €100 billion funding instrument under the Clean Industrial Deal to accelerate decarbonization of energy-intensive industries such as steel, cement, chemicals, and refining. It aims to mobilize public and private capital for low-carbon technologies, infrastructure, and projects, addressing fragmentation in existing EU funding mechanisms.

Key elements include:
- Funding sources: Innovation Fund resources, ETS revenues, and InvestEU revisions, to be governed under the future Competitiveness Fund.
- Pilot phase: €1 billion auction launched in 2025/2026 for decarbonizing key industrial processes, serving as a test for the full IDB.
- Focus areas: Process heat electrification, carbon capture, hydrogen use, and shared infrastructure to support project readiness and demand for low-carbon products.

The IDB responds to competitiveness challenges highlighted in the Draghi report and aligns with the Industrial Accelerator Act. It seeks to bridge the gap between innovation and deployment by providing coordinated, long-term support.

For more details, see the [European Commission announcement](https://single-market-economy.ec.europa.eu/industry/sustainability/net-zero-industry-act/net-zero-navigator/list-instruments/industrial-decarbonisation-bank_en) and related analyses from [Clean Air Task Force](https://www.catf.us/2026/04/making-100-billion-count-turning-europes-industrial-decarbonisation-ambitions-steel-ground/) and [Hydrogen Europe](https://hydrogeneurope.eu/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/Industrial-Decarbonisation-Bank-HE_Position-Paper_May-2026.pdf).
