# Michigan Industrial Decarbonization Roadmap and MHC Challenge (2026)

Michigan is accelerating industrial decarbonization as part of the MI Healthy Climate Plan, which targets a 100% carbon-neutral economy by 2050. Industries, along with power grids and transportation, are major sources of greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions in the state. The Michigan Department of Environment, Great Lakes, and Energy (EGLE) is spearheading efforts to reduce industrial emissions while fostering economic growth and manufacturing competitiveness. [Michigan EGLE Press Release, March 2026](https://www.michigan.gov/egle/newsroom/press-releases/2026/03/12/michigan-moves-forward-on-industrial-decarbonization-with-new-roadmap)

## Industrial Decarbonization Roadmap

EGLE has partnered with 5 Lakes Energy, a Michigan-based consulting firm, to develop a detailed roadmap providing policy recommendations for industrial GHG reductions. Key focuses include:

- Promoting economic growth through competitive, low-carbon manufacturing practices.
- Identifying essential tools, resources, and incentives to speed up decarbonization in response to rising global demand for low-carbon products.
- Aligning with MI Healthy Climate Plan priorities, such as establishing innovation hubs for cleaner technologies, offering incentives for energy efficiency, and providing technical assistance for process improvements.

The roadmap aims to drive clean energy progress in industry, support manufacturing innovation, and deliver benefits to communities and businesses statewide. [Michigan EGLE Press Release, March 2026](https://www.michigan.gov/egle/newsroom/press-releases/2026/03/12/michigan-moves-forward-on-industrial-decarbonization-with-new-roadmap)

## MI Healthy Climate Challenge (MHC Challenge)

As a core funding mechanism, the MI Healthy Climate Challenge supports industrial decarbonization projects. On April 21, 2026, Governor Gretchen Whitmer announced MHC Challenge #4: Go Big, Go Clean, a $1.8 million grant competition to fund facility-level feasibility studies. These studies will help Michigan’s industrial sector identify practical, cost-effective strategies to reduce GHG emissions, enhance competitiveness in global markets for low-emission products, and address barriers to adoption. [Michigan EGLE Press Release, April 2026](https://www.michigan.gov/egle/newsroom/press-releases/2026/04/21/industrial-decarbonization)

### Grant Details
- **Total Funding:** $1.8 million
- **Eligibility:** Michigan-based industrial companies, nonprofits, universities, research institutions, and technical or consulting firms partnering with Michigan industrial facilities.
- **Purpose:** Provide actionable information on efficiency opportunities, investment decisions, and emission reduction barriers to support decarbonization.
- **Application Process:**
  - Letters of Intent due by 11:59 p.m. (EDT) on May 19, 2026.
  - Full applications due by 11:59 p.m. (EDT) on July 7, 2026.
  - Informational webinar: April 28, 2026, at 1:30 p.m. (EDT).

This program aligns with Michigan's MI Healthy Climate Plan for a carbon-neutral economy by 2050 and builds on prior MHC Challenges by focusing on industrial feasibility studies. [Michigan EGLE Press Release, April 2026](https://www.michigan.gov/egle/newsroom/press-releases/2026/04/21/industrial-decarbonization)

## Industry Engagement and Feedback

EGLE's Office of Climate and Energy (OCE) has launched an Industrial Efficiency and Cost Savings Needs Assessment Survey to gather input from manufacturers on energy efficiency, operational improvements, and decarbonization initiatives. The survey includes opportunities for feedback on the MHC Challenge. Submissions by March 31, 2026, directly shaped the grant program, while ongoing input informs broader state policies. Participation is open to all and does not affect eligibility for MHC Challenge funding. [Michigan EGLE Press Release, March 2026](https://www.michigan.gov/egle/newsroom/press-releases/2026/03/12/michigan-moves-forward-on-industrial-decarbonization-with-new-roadmap)

## Strategic Importance

These initiatives position Michigan at the forefront of U.S. state-level industrial decarbonization, emphasizing collaboration between government, industry, and consultants. By integrating policy, funding, and stakeholder input, Michigan aims to achieve emission reductions that support net-zero goals without compromising its manufacturing economy.
