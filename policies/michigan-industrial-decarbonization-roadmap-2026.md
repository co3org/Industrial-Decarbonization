# Michigan Industrial Decarbonization Roadmap and MHC Challenge (2026)

Michigan is accelerating industrial decarbonization as part of the MI Healthy Climate Plan, which targets a 100% carbon-neutral economy by 2050. Industries, along with power grids and transportation, are major sources of greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions in the state. The Michigan Department of Environment, Great Lakes, and Energy (EGLE) is spearheading efforts to reduce industrial emissions while fostering economic growth and manufacturing competitiveness.[Michigan EGLE Press Release](https://www.michigan.gov/egle/newsroom/press-releases/2026/03/12/michigan-moves-forward-on-industrial-decarbonization-with-new-roadmap)

## Industrial Decarbonization Roadmap

EGLE has partnered with 5 Lakes Energy, a Michigan-based consulting firm, to develop a detailed roadmap providing policy recommendations for industrial GHG reductions. Key focuses include:

- Promoting economic growth through competitive, low-carbon manufacturing practices.
- Identifying essential tools, resources, and incentives to speed up decarbonization in response to rising global demand for low-carbon products.
- Aligning with MI Healthy Climate Plan priorities, such as establishing innovation hubs for cleaner technologies, offering incentives for energy efficiency, and providing technical assistance for process improvements.

The roadmap aims to drive clean energy progress in industry, support manufacturing innovation, and deliver benefits to communities and businesses statewide.[Michigan EGLE Press Release](https://www.michigan.gov/egle/newsroom/press-releases/2026/03/12/michigan-moves-forward-on-industrial-decarbonization-with-new-roadmap)

## MI Healthy Climate Challenge (MHC Challenge)

As a core funding mechanism, the next MHC Challenge will target industrial decarbonization projects. This grant program represents Michigan's push toward climate leadership in industry, funding practical solutions that cut emissions and bolster manufacturing competitiveness. Details on application processes and funding amounts are expected to be announced soon, with grants proposed for fiscal year 2026.[Michigan EGLE Press Release](https://www.michigan.gov/egle/newsroom/press-releases/2026/03/12/michigan-moves-forward-on-industrial-decarbonization-with-new-roadmap)

## Industry Engagement and Feedback

EGLE's Office of Climate and Energy (OCE) has launched an Industrial Efficiency and Cost Savings Needs Assessment Survey to gather input from manufacturers on energy efficiency, operational improvements, and decarbonization initiatives. The survey includes opportunities for feedback on the upcoming MHC Challenge. Submissions by March 31, 2026, will directly shape the grant program, while later input will inform broader state policies. Participation is open to all and does not affect eligibility for MHC Challenge funding.[Michigan EGLE Press Release](https://www.michigan.gov/egle/newsroom/press-releases/2026/03/12/michigan-moves-forward-on-industrial-decarbonization-with-new-roadmap)

## Strategic Importance

These initiatives position Michigan at the forefront of U.S. state-level industrial decarbonization, emphasizing collaboration between government, industry, and consultants. By integrating policy, funding, and stakeholder input, Michigan aims to achieve emission reductions that support net-zero goals without compromising its manufacturing economy.
