# Canada-Alberta Industrial Carbon Pricing Agreement (2026)

**Announcement Date:** May 13, 2026

## Overview

The Government of Canada and the Province of Alberta announced a landmark agreement on industrial carbon pricing, resolving long-standing tensions between federal climate policies and provincial economic priorities. This deal establishes a framework to increase the carbon price for large industrial emitters to $130 per tonne of CO2 equivalent by 2030, providing a stable signal for decarbonization investments in Alberta's energy-intensive sectors.[The Globe and Mail](https://www.theglobeandmail.com/politics/article-ottawa-alberta-industrial-carbon-pricing-oil-pipeline-smith-carney)

## Key Details

- **Pricing Mechanism:** The agreement builds on Canada's federal output-based pricing system (OBPS), with phased increases in the carbon levy for emissions-intensive industries such as oil and gas, cement, and steel.
- **Timeline:** Implementation begins immediately, with the price reaching $130/tonne by 2030, aligning with national net-zero targets.[Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/sustainability/climate-energy/canada-alberta-close-carbon-price-agreement-sources-say-2026-04-27)
- **Exemptions and Support:** Provisions include competitiveness adjustments for trade-exposed sectors and linkages to federal funding for clean technology adoption, potentially including CCUS incentives under the federal budget.

## Implications for Industrial Decarbonization

This accord is poised to drive significant emissions reductions in Alberta, Canada's largest industrial emitter, by incentivizing shifts to low-carbon fuels, electrification, and carbon capture technologies. It could mobilize billions in private investment for projects like hydrogen production and direct air capture, while supporting job transitions in the energy sector. The deal also paves the way for enhanced federal-provincial collaboration on broader decarbonization initiatives.[BNN Bloomberg](https://www.bnnbloomberg.ca/business/economics/2026/05/13/canada-alberta-likely-to-announce-industrial-carbon-price-deal-friday-sources)
