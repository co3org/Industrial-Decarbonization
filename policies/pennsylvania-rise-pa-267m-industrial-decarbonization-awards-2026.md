# Pennsylvania RISE PA $267M Awards for Industrial Decarbonization (April 2026)

## Overview
On April 28, 2026, the Shapiro Administration announced more than $267 million in funding for 31 manufacturing projects across Pennsylvania under the Reducing Industrial Sector Emissions in Pennsylvania (RISE PA) program. This investment, part of the $396 million federal grant from the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency’s Climate Pollution Reduction Grants, aims to reduce greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions, lower energy costs, and create jobs in the industrial sector, which accounts for over 30% of Pennsylvania's total annual GHG emissions.[Shapiro Administration Invests $267M](https://www.pa.gov/governor/newsroom/2026-press-releases/shapiro-administration-invests--267m-to-cut-pollution--create-jo)

The awards support medium-scale (MAT) and large-scale (LAT) projects demonstrating energy-efficient upgrades, including electrification, low-carbon fuel switching, carbon capture, and renewable energy integration.

## Key Impacts
- **Emission Reductions**: The projects are expected to reduce more than 1.3 million metric tons of CO2 equivalents in the first year, equivalent to removing 320,614 cars from the road or eliminating emissions from 154,666,592 gallons of gasoline.
- **Energy Savings**: Businesses will save over $3.1 million annually in energy costs.
- **Job Creation**: Thousands of good-paying jobs in construction and manufacturing.

> “As energy costs continue to rise... my Administration is helping Pennsylvania businesses lower their energy bills, create jobs, and reduce harmful air pollution,” said Governor Josh Shapiro.[Shapiro Administration Invests $267M](https://www.pa.gov/governor/newsroom/2026-press-releases/shapiro-administration-invests--267m-to-cut-pollution--create-jo)

## Eligible Measures and Project Examples
Projects focus on reducing emissions from fuel burning, equipment leaks, and chemical reactions. Key technologies include:

- **Electrification**: PECO Energy Company received $52 million to replace combustion turbines with electrified systems.
- **Renewable Energy**: G&S Foods LLC ($4.07 million for 4.07 MW solar with battery storage); High Concrete Group LLC ($1.9 million for 1.9 MW solar).
- **Energy Efficiency**: Gautier Steel Holdings Inc. ($1.89 million for reheat furnace upgrades, reducing 7,800 metric tons CO2e in year one). “This project will drive a vital investment in innovative technology... while significantly reducing the carbon emissions generated by our facility,” said Dale Gray, President & CEO.[Shapiro Administration Invests $267M](https://www.pa.gov/governor/newsroom/2026-press-releases/shapiro-administration-invests--267m-to-cut-pollution--create-jo)
- **Anaerobic Digesters and CHP**: Alouette Cheese USA ($44.9 million for digester and electrification); Nestle Purina Petcare Co. ($26.3 million for digester and heat recovery).
- **Carbon Capture**: Castle Builders Supply ($6.7 million).
- **Methane Capture**: CNX Green Ventures LLC ($31.5 million for gob ventilation boreholes); Iron Senergy Holding LLC ($4.8 million for Regenerative Thermal Oxidizer).[Pennsylvania awards $267M](https://www.bizjournals.com/pittsburgh/news/2026/04/28/cnx-industrial-redevelopment-coal-mine-methane.html)
- **Fuel Switching and Waste Heat Recovery**: Elliott Company ($2 million for lower-emitting gas); Kanin Energy, Inc. (3 MW waste heat-to-power system).

## Program Details
- **Total Program Funding**: $396 million (secured July 2024), with $267.8 million awarded to 31 projects.
- **Remaining Funding**: $52 million for next round of MAT and LAT grants; applications open May 15, 2026.
- **RISE PA Program Link**: [RISE PA Overview](https://www.pa.gov/agencies/dep/programs-and-services/energy-programs-office/rise-pa)

This builds on the initial RISE PA grant program and provides concrete examples of scalable decarbonization in manufacturing, supporting Pennsylvania's path to net-zero.
