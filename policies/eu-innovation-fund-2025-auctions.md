# EU Innovation Fund 2025 Auctions: €9.8 Billion in Bids for Clean Industry Projects

## Overview
The European Commission's Innovation Fund 2025 auctions, focused on industrial heat decarbonization and low-carbon hydrogen production, received overwhelming interest from the industrial sector. Closed on February 19, 2026, the auctions attracted 143 applications totaling nearly €10 billion in requested funding. This reflects strong momentum toward scaling breakthrough technologies in hard-to-abate industries, aligning with the EU's Clean Industrial Deal and goals for climate neutrality by 2050.

## Key Details
- **Total Bids**: €9.8 billion, broken down as €1.4 billion for the Industrial Heat Decarbonization auction (IF25 Heat) and €8.4 billion for the Hydrogen Production auction (IF25 Hydrogen).
- **Number of Applications**: 85 for IF25 Heat and 58 for IF25 Hydrogen.
- **Evaluation and Awards**: Ongoing assessment by independent experts; expected results in May-June 2026, with final grant agreements by Q4 2026.
- **Anticipated Impact**: Successful projects could deliver 3.78 million tonnes of CO₂-equivalent annual emissions reductions, 19.40 TWh of thermal energy savings over five years, 981 MW of electrified thermal capacity, and 4.3 GW of electrolyzer capacity for green hydrogen.

## Focus Areas
### Industrial Heat Decarbonization (IF25 Heat)
This auction targets innovative solutions to replace fossil fuels in high-temperature industrial processes, such as electric boilers, heat pumps, and thermal storage systems. Eligible projects must demonstrate high emissions reductions and support energy-intensive sectors like chemicals, steel, and cement.

### Hydrogen Production (IF25 Hydrogen)
Aimed at large-scale electrolyzers for green hydrogen, this supports feedstock for industries like ammonia production and refining. Projects must achieve at least 250 MW capacity and integrate with renewable energy sources to enhance Europe's energy independence.

## Implications for Industrial Decarbonization
These auctions underscore the EU's commitment to derisking clean technologies, boosting competitiveness in global low-carbon markets, and addressing barriers like high upfront costs. By prioritizing projects in disadvantaged regions, they also promote just transitions. The high bid volume signals investor confidence but highlights the need for streamlined permitting and supply chain development to realize full potential.

Stakeholders should monitor upcoming award announcements and explore synergies with other EU programs like the Net-Zero Industry Act. For application insights, refer to the [Innovation Fund guidelines](https://cinea.ec.europa.eu/programmes/innovation-fund_en).
