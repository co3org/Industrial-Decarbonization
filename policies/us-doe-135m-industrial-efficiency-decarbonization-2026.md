# US DOE $135 Million Funding for Industrial Efficiency and Decarbonization (2026)

## Overview

In April 2026, the U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) announced $135 million in funding for 40 innovative projects across 21 states. These selections under the Industrial Efficiency and Decarbonization Funding Opportunity Announcement (FOA DE-FOA-0002804) aim to reduce the carbon footprint of the industrial sector by advancing transformational technologies and processes. The initiative supports DOE's broader goal of achieving a net-zero emissions economy by 2050, focusing on energy-intensive industries where decarbonization can have the greatest impact.[^1][^2]

## Key Details

- **Funding Amount**: $135 million total, with individual awards varying based on project scope.
- **Number of Awards**: 40 projects selected from competitive applications.
- **Announcement Date**: April 23, 2026 (selections building on the September 2023 FOA launch, with updates in 2026).
- **Administering Office**: Industrial Efficiency and Decarbonization Office (IEDO) within DOE's Industrial Technologies Office.
- **Project Duration**: Up to 4 years, with a focus on demonstration and deployment.
- **Expected Impact**: Projects are projected to reduce industrial greenhouse gas emissions through innovations in energy efficiency, electrification, low-carbon fuels, and carbon capture, utilization, and storage (CCUS).[^1]

## Focus Areas and Pathways

The funding aligns with the four industrial decarbonization pathways outlined in DOE's Industrial Decarbonization Roadmap:

1. **Energy Efficiency**: Enhancing process efficiency in manufacturing to minimize energy use.
2. **Electrification**: Integrating electric technologies for heating, motors, and other industrial applications.
3. **Low-Carbon Fuels and Feedstocks**: Developing hydrogen, biomass, and renewable natural gas alternatives.
4. **Carbon Capture, Utilization, and Storage (CCUS)**: Capturing and sequestering emissions from hard-to-abate sectors.[^2]

Selected projects target high-volume chemicals production, metals, cement, and other energy-intensive subsectors, emphasizing unit operations improvements to lower emissions intensity.[^1]

## Examples of Funded Projects

While full details are forthcoming, initial highlights include:
- Innovations in electrochemical processes for chemical manufacturing to reduce fossil fuel dependency.
- Advanced heat recovery systems for metals processing.
- Pilot-scale CCUS integrations in cement production.

These projects build on prior DOE efforts and are expected to catalyze private investment, with total project costs exceeding $300 million when including cost-sharing.[^2]

## Application and Next Steps

The original FOA (DE-FOA-0002804) was open to industry, universities, national labs, and consortia. Selected recipients must demonstrate progress toward commercialization. Stakeholders interested in similar opportunities should monitor DOE's IEDO for future FOAs in FY2026.[^1]

## Relevance to Industrial Decarbonization

This funding accelerates the transition in hard-to-abate industries, addressing barriers like high upfront costs and technology maturity. It complements state-level grants and EU initiatives covered elsewhere in this repository, providing a U.S. federal perspective on scalable solutions.[^2]
