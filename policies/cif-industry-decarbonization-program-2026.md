# CIF Industry Decarbonization Program 2026

The Climate Investment Funds (CIF) launched a $1 billion Industry Decarbonization Program in 2026 to support heavy industry decarbonization in developing countries through concessional finance, investment plans, and green product competitiveness. [Source](https://www.cif.org/news/seven-countries-developing-investment-plans-decarbonize-heavy-industry)

## Overview

- **Objective**: Accelerate decarbonization of hard-to-abate sectors (steel, cement, chemicals, mining) in emerging economies while building long-term industrial competitiveness.
- **Participating Countries** (first cohort): Brazil, Egypt, Mexico, Namibia, South Africa, Türkiye, Uzbekistan.
- **Funding Mechanism**: Blended finance, technical assistance, and dedicated streams for clean technology deployment.
- **Timeline**: Endorsements and investment plan development ongoing as of May 2026; initial country plans advancing rapidly.

## Key Components

- Support for country-specific investment plans targeting emissions reductions in heavy industry.
- Focus on generating demand for green industrial products to drive global markets.
- Integration with broader clean tech markets projected to exceed $2 trillion by 2035.

## Relevance to Industrial Decarbonization

This program addresses a critical gap in global coverage by targeting developing nations where industrial growth is rapid and financing barriers are high. It complements existing policies in the repo (e.g., India CCUS, Vietnam VIDA) by providing a multilateral framework for concessional capital.
