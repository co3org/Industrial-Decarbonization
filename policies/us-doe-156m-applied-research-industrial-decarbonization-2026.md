# US DOE $156M Funding Opportunity for Applied Research and Development Projects to Drive Industrial Decarbonization (April 2026)

## Overview
The U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) announced a $156 million funding opportunity on April 23, 2026, to support applied research, development, and demonstration (RD&D) projects aimed at reducing greenhouse gas emissions in the industrial sector. Managed by the Industrial Efficiency and Decarbonization Office (IEDO) within the Office of Energy Efficiency and Renewable Energy (EERE), this initiative seeks to advance innovative technologies that improve energy efficiency, revitalize U.S. manufacturing, create jobs, and enhance public health.

The industrial sector accounts for approximately 33% of U.S. primary energy consumption and 30% of energy-related CO₂ emissions. Achieving net-zero emissions by 2050 requires transformative strategies across diverse industrial processes. This funding builds on the 2022 Industrial Decarbonization Roadmap and complements ongoing programs like the $6 billion Industrial Demonstrations Program and the Industrial Heat Shot™, which aims for at least 85% lower emissions in industrial heat by 2035.[^1]

The opportunity is part of the Technologies for Industrial Emissions Reduction Development (TIEReD) Program, which integrates efforts in fundamental science, RD&D, pilot demonstrations, and workforce development.

## Key Funding Areas
The funding covers eight topic areas focused on high-impact decarbonization technologies:

- **Topic 1: Industrial Heat** – Development of equipment for efficient thermal processes, including electrification, low/no-heat alternatives, and industrial heat pumps.
- **Topic 2: Low-Carbon Fuels Utilization RD&D** – Technologies for using low-carbon fuels like hydrogen, including combustion systems, hydrogen-based processes, and flexible combined heat and power.
- **Topic 3: Exploratory Cross-Sector R&D** –
  - Subtopic 3a: Enabling Flexible Industrial Energy Use – Transformational tech for renewable integration, load flexibility, and thermal energy storage.
  - Subtopic 3b: Enhanced Thermal Conductivity Materials – Advanced materials for electrified thermal equipment (co-funded by Advanced Materials and Manufacturing Technologies Office).
- **Topic 4: Chemicals** – Innovations for high-volume chemicals, including advanced separations, reactor systems, and dynamic catalysts to reduce CO₂ emissions.
- **Topic 5: Iron and Steel** – Energy efficiency improvements, innovative manufacturing, electrification, hydrogen utilization, and scrap management.
- **Topic 6: Food and Beverage Products** – Low/zero-carbon solutions for heating, cooling, and refrigeration in energy-intensive operations.
- **Topic 7: Cement and Concrete** – Addressing process emissions through supplementary cementitious materials, novel production, low-carbon formulations, and CO₂ mineralization.
- **Topic 8: Forest Products** – Efficiency gains in drying, paper forming, and pulping processes.

## Eligibility and Application Process
Eligible applicants include U.S. entities such as businesses, universities, nonprofits, and tribal organizations. Awards will be issued as cooperative agreements with a performance period of approximately three years.

- **Concept Papers Due**: April 17, 2026, 5:00 PM ET
- **Full Applications Due**: June 23, 2026, 5:00 PM ET

Applications must be submitted via the EERE Exchange portal. For more details, visit the official funding opportunity announcement.[^1]

This funding represents a significant step in accelerating industrial decarbonization, targeting sectors responsible for a substantial portion of emissions and fostering innovation to meet 2050 net-zero goals.
