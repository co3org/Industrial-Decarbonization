# UNIDO NDC 3.0 Guidebook for Industrial Decarbonization

The United Nations Industrial Development Organization (UNIDO) released the NDC 3.0 Guidebook for Industrial Decarbonization (Volume 2) in October 2025. This practical, implementation-focused resource supports governments in enhancing the ambition and implementation of industrial decarbonization measures within Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs). [https://decarbonization.unido.org/resources/ndc-3-0-guidebook-for-industrial-decarbonization/](https://decarbonization.unido.org/resources/ndc-3-0-guidebook-for-industrial-decarbonization/)

## Overview and Structure

The guidebook provides a structured framework with five building blocks and a curated list of industrial decarbonization measures across material efficiency, energy efficiency, fuel switching, process innovation, and carbon management. It includes a decision tree for policy options, regulations, and technological measures. [https://www.unido.org/sites/default/files/unido-publications/2025-10/ndc-guidebook-vol2.pdf](https://www.unido.org/sites/default/files/unido-publications/2025-10/ndc-guidebook-vol2.pdf)

It targets heavy industries and emphasizes practical pathways for maximum impact in both developed and developing countries.

## Relevance to Industrial Decarbonization

This complements existing repository resources like the IEA Policy Toolbox by offering NDC-specific guidance, including integration with national climate plans, stakeholder engagement, and monitoring frameworks. It highlights roles for circular economy approaches and resilient industrial systems.

## Key Recommendations

Governments should prioritize policy mixes that combine regulatory standards, financial incentives, and technology deployment while addressing competitiveness and just transition aspects. The guidebook supports identification of high-impact measures tailored to national contexts.

*Source: UNIDO NDC 3.0 Guidebook for Industrial Decarbonization (2025).*
