# Guidelines for Decarbonizing Industry to Meet Global Climate Goals

Published on April 10, 2026, by the Center on Global Energy Policy at Columbia University, this report outlines strategies to triple the rate of industrial decarbonization from approximately 2% per year to at least 6.1% per year, aligning with Paris Agreement goals for net-zero emissions by 2050 in high-income countries (including China) and 2060 in emerging markets and developing economies (EMDEs).

## Key Challenges and Scenarios
Industrial sectors, responsible for about 25% of global CO2 emissions, face barriers like high costs for low-carbon technologies and policy fragmentation. The report models scenarios showing that without accelerated action, industrial emissions could remain flat or rise, undermining climate targets. To achieve near-zero emitting (NZE) facilities, all new heavy industry plants must be built as NZE by 2030 in high-income countries and China, and by 2040 in EMDEs, while phasing out higher-emission facilities by 2050/2060.

## Main Recommendations
- **Carbon Pricing and Regulations**: Implement effective carbon prices of ~US$250 per ton of CO2e in high-income countries and China, and $125 per ton in EMDEs. Combine with regulatory standards for GHG emissions or electrification, and subsidies like tax credits to bridge cost gaps.
- **Remove Market Barriers**: Update codes and processes to enable adoption of commercialized technologies, such as cementitious material substitution and addressing pricing disparities between fossil fuels and clean electricity.
- **Support First NZE Plants**: Use lead markets with policies like green procurement, sectoral subsidies, and tradable low-emissions requirements to build initial NZE facilities, reducing risks and driving innovation. Estimated additional costs: $88 billion/year in capital (2.7% of 2024 energy investments) and $171 billion/year in operations for clean electricity, hydrogen, and CCUS.
- **Border Adjustments and Standards**: Apply border GHG standards or carbon adjustments to ensure importers face equivalent policies, protecting domestic clean investments. Set standards for industrial inputs (e.g., low-GHG electricity, hydrogen, or CCUS with >90% capture rates).
- **Financing Mechanisms**: Develop thematic green bonds and production/investment tax credits to address upfront and operating cost gaps.

## Sectors and Technologies Covered
The guidelines focus on heavy industries including steel, cement, chemicals, and ammonia, emphasizing pathways like material efficiency, recycling, direct reduction of iron, electric heating, low-carbon feedstocks, hydrogen, and CCUS. Partial mitigations (e.g., efficiency, electrification) are recommended where full NZE is not yet viable.

This report complements existing resources by providing a global policy framework to operationalize decarbonization, emphasizing the need for coordinated international action.[^1]
