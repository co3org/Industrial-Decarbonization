# IEA Policy Toolbox for Industrial Decarbonisation

The International Energy Agency (IEA) released the Policy Toolbox for Industrial Decarbonisation in March 2025 as a comprehensive repository of policy instruments to help governments design and implement strategies for reducing emissions in heavy industry. [https://www.iea.org/reports/policy-toolbox-for-industrial-decarbonisation](https://www.iea.org/reports/policy-toolbox-for-industrial-decarbonisation)

## Overview and Structure

The toolbox builds on the IEA's earlier 2022 work on net-zero heavy industry sectors in G7 countries. It organizes policy instruments into three broad areas with nine categories:

- **Framework fundamentals**: Establishing long-term GHG reduction plans and mobilising finance/investment.
- **Targeted actions**: Managing existing assets, creating markets for near-zero emissions materials, developing technologies, and accelerating material efficiency/circularity.
- **Enabling conditions**: International cooperation, infrastructure planning, and progress tracking. [https://iea.blob.core.windows.net/assets/cfd54d6e-fdf2-4582-83de-31d651c4cb14/PolicyToolboxforIndustrialDecarbonisation.pdf](https://iea.blob.core.windows.net/assets/cfd54d6e-fdf2-4582-83de-31d651c4cb14/PolicyToolboxforIndustrialDecarbonisation.pdf)

It includes global examples of policies applied in both developed and emerging economies, serving as a practical guide for Climate Club members and others.

## Relevance to Industrial Decarbonization

This resource provides a structured, evidence-based framework that complements sector-specific and country-specific policies already documented in the repository. It emphasizes policy mixes for competitiveness, innovation, and just transitions, addressing gaps in integrating circular economy and socio-economic considerations.

## Key Recommendations

Governments should strengthen linkages between decarbonization pathways, enhance international cooperation for level playing fields, and improve data tracking. The toolbox highlights instruments like carbon contracts for difference, public procurement for low-carbon materials, and R&D support.

*Source: IEA Policy Toolbox for Industrial Decarbonisation (2025).*
