# 2024 Industrial Decarbonization Market Outlook

## Executive Summary

The industrial decarbonization sector is experiencing steady growth amid policy support and technological advancements, with the global market projected to expand from US$2.47 billion in 2024 to US$3.30 billion by 2030 at a CAGR of 4.9% [The Future of Industrial Decarbonization - ABI Research](https://www.abiresearch.com/blogs/2024/09/12/the-future-of-industrial-decarbonization/). BloombergNEF's bi-annual Industrial Decarbonization Market Outlook highlights progress in scaling low-carbon alternatives for high-emission sectors like steel, aluminum, petrochemicals, and cement, driven by incentives from the Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) and international commitments [Industry Decarbonization Market Outlook 1H 2024 - BloombergNEF](https://about.bnef.com/insights/industry-and-buildings/industry-decarbonization-market-outlook-1h-2024/). However, challenges persist, including project cancellations totaling $17 billion in the US between 2018 and Q3 2025, particularly in hydrogen and clean fuels [Industrial decarbonization investments are struggling - Latitude Media](https://www.latitudemedia.com/news/industrial-decarbonization-investments-are-struggling/).

Key trends include expanding adoption of low-carbon steel and cement, growth in advanced plastic recycling, and relief for carbon storage projects through policy adjustments [Industrial Decarbonization: Things to Watch 2024 - BloombergNEF](https://about.bnef.com/insights/industry-and-buildings/industrial-decarbonization-things-to-watch-2024/). The World Economic Forum's Net-Zero Industry Tracker projects a 41% decrease in absolute direct emissions from industrial sectors (steel, cement, aluminum, primary chemicals, and oil & gas) by 2030, underscoring the need for accelerated innovation [Net-Zero Industry Tracker 2024 | World Economic Forum](https://www.weforum.org/publications/net-zero-industry-tracker-2024/cross-sector-findings-net-zero-tracker-2024/).

## Sector-Specific Advances

### Steel and Cement
Low-carbon steel production is gaining traction with breakthroughs in hydrogen-based direct reduction and electrification. For instance, projects like HYBRIT in Sweden demonstrate scalable green steel using renewable hydrogen, reducing emissions by up to 95% [Decarbonizing U.S. industry: Progress and opportunities](https://www.catf.us/2024/08/decarbonizing-us-industry-progress-opportunities/). In cement, carbon capture utilization and storage (CCUS) is pivotal; Norway's Norcem Brevik plant, set for operation in 2024, will capture 400,000 tonnes of CO2 annually, representing a major step in sector-wide adoption [Industrial Decarbonization: Things to Watch 2024 - BloombergNEF](https://about.bnef.com/insights/industry-and-buildings/industrial-decarbonization-things-to-watch-2024/).

### Chemicals and Petrochemicals
Electrification and bio-based feedstocks are emerging pathways. The US leads with 90 commercial-scale net-zero aligned projects, including low-carbon ammonia production via electrolysis [Mapping the Path to Industrial Decarbonization - RMI](https://rmi.org/mapping-the-path-to-industrial-decarbonization/). Despite this, global progress lags in developing countries, where access to green hydrogen and CCUS remains limited [Global Industrial Decarbonization Progress Leaves Developing Countries Behind](https://iesr.or.id/en/global-industrial-decarbonization-progress-leaves-developing-countries-behind/).

### Aluminum and Other Metals
In aluminum, inert anode technology promises emission-free production by eliminating carbon anodes. Pilot projects funded under the IRA are advancing, with potential for 50% emissions reduction by 2030 [Five pathways to industrial decarbonization - CAS](https://www.cas.org/resources/cas-insights/industrial-decarbonization).

## Policy and Investment Landscape

The US Department of Energy's Industrial Decarbonization Roadmap emphasizes aligning heat decarbonization with future infrastructure, including new low-carbon technologies [Industrial Decarbonization Roadmap and Context](https://www.energy.gov/sites/default/files/2024-12/itiac-march-2024-cresko-industrial-decarb-roadmap.pdf). OECD's green industrial policies highlight fiscal support for net-zero transitions, with countries like the US and EU deploying subsidies and tax credits totaling hundreds of billions [Green industrial policies for the net-zero transition | OECD](https://www.oecd.org/content/dam/oecd/en/publications/reports/2024/10/green-industrial-policies-for-the-net-zero-transition_1e066699/ccc326d3-en.pdf).

Energy efficiency remains a foundational pillar, potentially reducing industrial emissions by up to 30% through retrofits and process optimizations, as per a Canadian study [Energy Efficiency as a Foundational Technology Pillar for Industrial Decarbonization](https://energyefficiency.ornl.gov/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/EE-for-Industrial-Decarb.pdf).

Investments face hurdles, with cancellations signaling the need for stable policy frameworks. The Industrial Innovation Initiative's 2024 Federal Policy Blueprint calls for enhanced R&D funding and deployment incentives [2024 Federal Policy Blueprint - Industrial Innovation Initiative](https://industrialinnovation.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/2024-i3-Blueprint.pdf).

## Challenges and Opportunities

While breakthroughs in CCUS, hydrogen, and electrification offer pathways to net-zero, scaling remains costly and uneven. The UNIDO highlights material innovations and low-emission hydrogen as essential for hard-to-abate sectors [Breakthrough technologies for industrial decarbonization - UNIDO](https://decarbonization.unido.org/solutions/breakthrough-technologies/). Opportunities lie in state-level planning, as analyzed by RMI, which recommends tailoring policies to local contexts and emphasizing co-benefits like job creation and air quality improvements [Powering Progress: Industrial Decarbonization Planning at the State Level](https://rmi.org/powering-progress-industrial-decarbonization-planning-at-the-state-level/).

## Conclusion

2024 marks a pivotal year for industrial decarbonization, with market growth and policy momentum countering investment risks. Sustained international collaboration is crucial to bridge gaps, particularly in developing regions, ensuring equitable progress toward global net-zero goals.
